Power tree · live rails · P = V × I (✓ = within 15%)i

The formula. Each rail exposes three SMC keys: voltage (V…), current (I…), and power (P…). Electrical power is P = V × I. The ✓ appears when a rail's own V × I lands within 15% of its reported P — proof we're decoding all three correctly off the live hardware. Two honest non-matches: SVR is an aggregate regulator (one V·I pair can't equal a sum of child rails), and gated rails like C42 read ~0 V because their voltage is sensed elsewhere.

What we've verified: the numbers — Watt's law holds, so the V/I/P decode is correct. What we have NOT: what each rail physically powers. Apple documents none of this; SVR, C02, E0b are the SMC's own opaque codes, not labels.

Best current inference (correlational — from which workload made each rail's power rise, not from schematics; medium confidence):
• C02 C42 C03 C43 E0b SVR rose under CPU load → CPU / SoC core rails
• C10 C12 C20 C22 C40 b0f rose under GPU load → GPU rails
• C32 P2b R2b R0b R9b rose under memory load → DRAM rails
• R8b rose under disk load → SSD rail

Exact device/pin mapping is still unknown — turning these inferences into ground truth is an open reverse-engineering task, same caveat as the temperature sensors.
